# Mariner Nexus Design System

## Core Philosophy
The design system must support:
- clarity
- control
- perceived value
- premium positioning
- conversion readiness
- scalable execution

This is not decorative design.
This is strategic design.

The design language should make Mariner Nexus feel:
- intelligent
- premium
- cinematic
- intentional
- modern
- differentiated

---

## Experience Model
The site combines:

- executive clarity (Linear)
- interactive intelligence (Active Theory)
- cinematic impact (RESN)
- premium product restraint (Apple / Vercel / Stripe)

The goal is not to imitate any one reference.
The goal is to synthesize their strengths into a cohesive Mariner Nexus experience.

---

## Color Palette

### Core
- Black: #0A0A0A
- White: #FFFFFF
- Soft White: #F5F7FA

### Accent
- Nexus Blue: #2563EB
- Glow Blue: #4DA3FF
- Core Energy: #7DD3FC

### Supporting
- Border: rgba(255,255,255,0.12)
- Glass: rgba(255,255,255,0.06)
- Glow Layer: rgba(37,99,235,0.18)

Use accent color with restraint.
It should feel like energy and intelligence, not decoration overload.

---

## Visual Language

### Principles
- dark-first premium interface
- strong contrast
- precise hierarchy
- minimal clutter
- restrained glow usage
- intelligent use of depth and atmosphere

### Materials
- glass cards
- soft glow edges
- subtle borders
- dark premium backgrounds
- layered blur and light cues where appropriate

### Avoid
- noisy gradients
- cyberpunk clichés
- fake futuristic UI
- overly bright or saturated effects
- visual clutter that weakens trust

---

## Typography

### Recommended Families
- Inter
- Geist
- Satoshi
- Manrope

### Typography Style
- clean
- geometric
- high contrast
- premium
- highly legible
- strong hierarchy

### Guidelines
- use large, declarative headlines
- keep supporting copy concise
- maintain clear spacing between heading, subheading, and body
- use scale to direct attention
- avoid dense text blocks

Typography should do a large part of the work.
Premium perception comes from discipline, not just visual effects.

---

## Layout Principles
- spacious
- grid-based
- minimal clutter
- every section must have purpose
- consistent rhythm
- strong compositional balance

Each section must:
- have a clear role
- be easy to scan
- guide the user forward
- feel composed rather than assembled

Avoid:
- arbitrary stacking
- cramped layouts
- equal-weight sections with no narrative hierarchy
- design patterns that feel template-generated

---

## Logo Usage

### Variants
- Full 3D logo → hero sections and major brand moments
- Simplified icon → UI and navigation
- Micro mark → favicon and very small applications
- Light version → documents and light surfaces where needed

### Minimum Sizes
- Full logo: 120px
- Icon: 32px
- Micro: 16px

### Clear Space
Minimum clear space: 50% of logo width

### Usage Rule
The logo should feel premium and intentional.
Do not over-repeat it or turn it into decoration.

---

## Motion Principles

Motion must feel:
- controlled
- intelligent
- premium
- responsive
- intentional

### Motion Should Do Three Things
1. guide attention
2. support storytelling
3. improve perception of quality

### Use
- soft fades
- subtle parallax
- glow pulses
- depth transitions
- staggered reveals
- layered motion between sections
- controlled hover states

### Avoid
- excessive movement
- gimmicky effects
- noisy animations
- motion that competes with reading
- motion added just to look advanced

Motion is a tool for pacing and emphasis, not spectacle for its own sake.

---

## Hero Animation
The hero is one of the most important perception layers on the site.

### Default Pattern
- static or lightweight visual fallback first
- motion loads after
- optional video or WebGL layer
- headline and CTA remain readable immediately

### Hero Goals
- establish premium tone
- create intrigue
- signal technical sophistication
- preserve clarity and performance

---

## Advanced Rendering Layer

Advanced rendering is allowed, but only when it supports the business and experiential goals of the site.

### Acceptable Technologies
- Three.js / React Three Fiber
- Canvas effects
- shader-based accent layers where appropriate

### Best Use Cases
- homepage hero
- isolated transition moments
- premium brand moments
- selective immersive case study sections

### Rules
- never block load
- always provide fallback
- limit to hero or key moments
- never reduce readability
- never interfere with primary CTA visibility
- simplify or disable on tablet/mobile when needed

### Preferred Philosophy
Use immersive rendering as a premium enhancement layer.
Do not make the site dependent on advanced rendering to feel high-end.

---

## Experience Intelligence

### Attention Engineering
Use contrast, scale, whitespace, hierarchy, lighting, and motion to direct focus.

Priority order for attention should usually be:
1. headline
2. supporting value statement
3. primary CTA
4. visual reinforcement
5. secondary CTA or supporting proof

### Perception Design
Use restraint, polish, spacing, and consistency to increase perceived value.

Premium perception comes from:
- calm confidence
- sharp hierarchy
- intentional pacing
- high-quality surfaces
- disciplined visual rhythm

### Performance Awareness
Keep immersive effects smooth and responsible.

Always balance:
- immersion
- readability
- responsiveness
- loading behavior
- device capability

---

## Surfaces and UI Treatment

### Preferred Surface Language
- translucent glass with restraint
- soft edge highlights
- subtle border definition
- deep background layering
- occasional metallic cues tied to brand energy

### UI Principles
- interface elements should feel tactile but refined
- buttons should feel premium, not oversized or playful
- cards should feel architectural, not generic

---

## Component Philosophy

Components must be:
- reusable
- scalable
- consistent
- modular
- purposeful

### Common Component Types
- hero systems
- proof / case study modules
- CTA sections
- feature and service layouts
- quote / credibility blocks
- logo and brand moments
- scheduling / contact embeds

### Avoid
- one-off components with no reuse value
- inconsistent corner radii, spacing, or shadows
- over-designed modules that clash with the system

---

## Interaction Design

### Buttons
- feel responsive
- use subtle state changes
- preserve readability and premium tone

### Hover States
- restrained
- intentional
- no exaggerated gimmicks

### Navigation
- smooth
- predictable
- premium
- supportive of the cinematic experience without becoming confusing

### Scroll Behavior
- should feel smooth and guided
- sections should reveal with intention
- pacing matters more than speed

---

## Responsive Behavior

### Desktop
- full cinematic experience
- layered motion
- immersive depth where appropriate
- strongest premium expression

### Tablet
- reduce heavy motion
- preserve hierarchy and pacing
- maintain premium feel with lighter execution

### Mobile
- prioritize speed and readability
- simplify advanced effects
- reduce immersive rendering when needed
- keep CTAs obvious and interactions intuitive

Responsive design is not only about resizing.
It is about preserving the intended feeling across device conditions.

---

## Light Mode Support
Light mode may be supported selectively where appropriate, but the system is dark-first.

If light mode is used:
- preserve premium contrast
- retain brand energy
- avoid flat corporate whiteness
- maintain the same level of polish and intentionality

---

## Constraints
- no clutter
- no generic templates
- no unnecessary motion
- no cheap-looking futurism
- no visual choices that reduce trust
- no effect-heavy sections with weak messaging
- every element must serve a purpose

---

## Final Objective
Create a design system where:

- the user feels guided
- the experience feels premium
- the visuals reinforce intelligence and trust
- the motion supports storytelling
- the site remains performant
- the system scales cleanly across future pages and case studies

Final perception:

“This is high-end, intentional, and built by experts.”
